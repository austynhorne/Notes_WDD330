Web Token(JWT)is an open standard that defines a compact and self-contained way for securely transmitting information between parties as a JSON object.
It is breaked up into three main parts the Header, the Payload, and the signature.
The header typically consists of two parts: the type of the token, which is JWT, and the hashing algorithm
The payload contains the claims. Claims are statements about an entity (typically, the user) and additional metadata.
The signature is used to verify that the sender of the JWT is who it says it is and to ensure that the message wasâ€™t changed in the way.
